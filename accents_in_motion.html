AccentsInMotion

Together with Kirsi Mustalahti, I performed through the use of expressive body movements, sensors and live coding (by Joseph Wilk & Andreas Bergsland) to control words and voice playback. This took place at #MTFLabs in Stockholm, 2018.

"AccentsInMotion's aim was to research new expression of performing art with aid of sensors and computer technology control one or several aspects of words, music with performers/ dancers body movement and physical presence. Two of the most important channels of human communication are voice/speech and bodily gestures/movements. Theatre and performance traditions have used these in artistic expressions for millennia. We wanted to explore this in a new way by letting dancers/performers ‘speak’ with their bodies using new technology that translates body movement to speech or speech-like sounds. All data was live streamed to control signal processing of the voice samples in real-time and live coding. The workgroup was established in Stockholm Music Tech Fest in September 2018 as a part of the Performance Labs AI led by Michela Magas. The team worked for one week to explore and develop the artistic possibilities of coupling expressive body-movements to a rich and varied speech database using sensor technology and live coding." - Music Tech Fest , 2018

"Lilian Jap and Kirsi Mustalahti, founder of ACC Global, performed a compelling piece with sounds that responded to their movements. Their stark actions gave them the appearance of puppets of terror, held in a vice-like grip by dark forces with ominous voices. " - Kitmonsters.com, 2018
