Communicative Pots & Pans

Winner of Best Concept for Somaesthetic Design by Electrolux Group Design ​

This project is a collaboration with Electrolux to find new product ideas, with the theme “somaesthetics”. Throughout the project, the focus has been on the reflective practices of interaction design and, hence, the design process has included various design workshops/sessions to explore our own experiences in somaesthetic practices. From a first-person perspective, me and my two collaborators comined our shared experiences and were in the later stage of the process reflected upon when mapping the design space, producing "magic machines", practically exploring with materials, lo-fi prototyping.

As cooking being one of our daily tasks, it can contribute to induce stress in our day-to-day life as it requires multple tasks to keep track of. One in particular was not knowing in which order to perform tasks. This resulted in our final concept idea of having an assistant in the kitchen which would help keep track of tasks. In the form of pots and pans, they are intended to communicate when their contents need your attention.

The final prototype consists of a pot which has a LED strip around its sides. When the pot wants to communicate to the user it does so by lighting up the LEDs in a slowly pulsating pattern. This concept was influenced by our takeaways from the different somaesthetic methods that we engaged with during the design process.
